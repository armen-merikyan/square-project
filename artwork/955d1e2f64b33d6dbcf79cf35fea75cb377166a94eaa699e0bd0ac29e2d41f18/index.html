Artist Statement

The emotional tone juxtaposes safety and exposure through a palette reflecting shelter and openness: warm brown as a grounding, protective layer, and the pale skin and cold white to signify vulnerability and exposure. The primary collision occurs in the top right corner, where dense colors (like warm brown) engage with the softness of pale skin and open blue, accentuating a feeling of tension. The overlapping patterns reflect the concept of pressure versus release, embodying the struggle between rigid safety and the fluidity of exposure.
