Artist Statement

The emotional tone reflects the struggle between safety and exposure, where the palette consists of soft greens and browns representing shelter and severity. The contrast occurs in the top-left, with warmer colors demanding attention, while a cooler, muted area emerges at the bottom-right, suggesting a release. The arrangement echoes the idea of a 'pressure corner' with dense marking, as though the higher density of lines on one side disrupts the stability of the calmer side, creating a rhythm of shelter versus exposure.
