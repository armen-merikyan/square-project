Artist Statement

The emotional tone reflects a tension between safety and exposure, marked by a dominant soft palette subtly interrupted by heavier, richer tones. The layout suggests an empty room filled with the potential for both vulnerability and shelter, where gentle greens indicate safety while sharp whites and blues symbolize exposure. The main contrast collides vertically, with softer fields at the left symbolizing protection and the more intense hues on the right representing external pressures. Broken pixels act as evidence of conflict, suggesting wear due to the emotional interplay—each color placement echoes the seed's essence. The piece uses open space effectively, allowing the viewer's imagination to resonate with the themes of pressure and release.
